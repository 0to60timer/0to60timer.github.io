What is 0-100 MPH Time?

The 0-100 mph acceleration time measures how quickly a vehicle can accelerate from a complete stop to 100 miles per hour. This metric is particularly important for high-performance and supercar testing, as it reveals sustained acceleration capability beyond the standard 0-60 mph test.

Why 0-100 MPH Matters

  • High-Performance Benchmark: Separates true performance cars from quick accelerators
  • Power Band Testing: Shows how well a car maintains acceleration at higher speeds
  • Gearing Evaluation: Tests transmission efficiency through multiple gears
  • Aerodynamic Impact: Reveals how drag affects acceleration at speed
üí° Key Insight: While many cars can achieve good 0-60 times, 0-100 mph separates vehicles with true high-performance capabilities. It requires sustained power delivery and efficient gearing.

0-60 vs 0-100: What's the Difference?

0-60 MPH

  • Tests initial acceleration
  • Usually completed in 1-2 gears
  • Launch technique critical
  • Less affected by aerodynamics

0-100 MPH

  • Tests sustained acceleration
  • Requires 3-4 gears typically
  • Power delivery throughout range
  • Aerodynamics become significant

How to Measure 0-100 MPH Time

Step 1: Safety and Location

  • Requires a long, straight stretch (at least 1 mile)
  • Closed course or track recommended
  • Never test on public roads
  • Ensure clear visibility and safe conditions

Step 2: Vehicle Preparation

  1. Check tire pressure
  2. Ensure adequate fuel
  3. Warm up engine and transmission
  4. Disable traction control if experienced
  5. Select appropriate drive mode

Step 3: Set Up 0to60Timer.com

  1. Visit 0to60Timer.com/tracker
  2. Grant location and motion permissions
  3. Enable "0-100 mph" in visible metrics
  4. Mount phone securely
  5. Wait for GPS signal

Step 4: Perform the Test

  1. Come to complete stop
  2. Verify 0 mph reading
  3. Launch with full throttle
  4. Shift at optimal points
  5. Maintain full throttle through 100 mph
  6. Time recorded automatically

Interpreting Your Results

Performance Categories

  • Hypercars: Under 5 seconds
  • Supercars: 5-8 seconds
  • High-Performance Sports Cars: 8-12 seconds
  • Performance Cars: 12-18 seconds
  • Standard Sports Cars: 18-25 seconds

What Affects 0-100 Time?

  • Horsepower: Sustained power output crucial
  • Torque Curve: Power delivery across RPM range
  • Weight: Power-to-weight ratio
  • Gearing: Transmission ratios and shift speed
  • Aerodynamics: Drag coefficient matters at high speed
  • Traction: Ability to put power down

Tips for Accurate Testing

  • Multiple Runs: Perform 3-5 runs for consistency
  • Optimal Shifts: Practice shift points
  • Consistent Launches: Same technique each time
  • Weather Conditions: Test in similar conditions
  • GPS Lock: Ensure strong satellite signal
